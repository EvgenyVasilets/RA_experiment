Instructions

Thank you for participating in our experiment on how people react to different decision situations. In the current study you will be shown 44 different lotteries and will be asked to make decisions about whether you wish to accept each lottery or not. These lotteries will offer you a positive and a negative outcome with equal probability (fifty-fifty). Note that gain and loss amounts will change for each new decision situation, so consider each lottery carefully before you make a decision. Your decisions will affect you bonus payments as outlined below. The overall duration of the study should be about 20-25 minutes.

The outcomes in these lotteries are shown as Experimental Currency Units (ECU). Each ECU is equivalent to 2.5p (0.025 Pounds). Because you can also incur losses, you have been endowed with 40 ECUs. At the end of the experiment, one of these lotteries will be randomly chosen. If you chose to accept the lottery on the payout relevant trial, it will be played out via a virtual coin flip. Depending on the outcome of the coin flip, the gains may be added to your initial endowment if the good state occurs (e.g., heads), or the losses may be subtracted from it if the bad state occurs (e.g., tails). Therefore, your decisions in the experiment together with the outcome of the coin flip will determine your bonus payment, which will be added to your participation fee.

In each trial, the lottery will be presented by showing you each outcome separately. These two outcomes are equally likely. You have a maximum of 6 seconds to make a decision, but you can do so quicker if you like. If you wish to accept the lottery, then press the upwards key (↑). In case you want to reject, press the downward key (↓). Once you have made your decision, you will receive feedback on your screen. In case you don't answer within 6 seconds, we will ask you to answer faster, and if that trial gets selected in the end, you will get the negative outcome for certain.

For example:

Let's say in the trial that is selected (one of the 44), the outcomes were 32 (Left) and -20 (Right).

 

If you decide to reject, then, your bonus payment will be zero.

If you decide to accept, then a random outcome is determined. If the random outcome is Left, you get 32.

If you decide to accept, then a random outcome is determined. If the random outcome is Right, you get -20.

  

Questions

Now we will ask you a couple of questions regarding the instructions in order to see if you understood correctly. After, we will show you 3 practice trials for you to get used to making decisions in the current experiment. The decisions on the next 3 trials will not count toward the final payment, they are simply there for you to try out how to make decisions in this experiment. After you completed the practice trials, we will notify you and the actual experiment will start, with decisions that can count towards your final payout. After you finish all the trials, a short survey will be presented. Once you finish the survey, the random trial will be selected and your bonus payment will be calculated.
